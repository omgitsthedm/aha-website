# After Hours Agenda — Brand Photoshoot Creative Brief

## Brand summary
After Hours Agenda is an independent NYC streetwear label built for people who keep working after the day job ends. Every piece is made to order, printed on demand, and shipped from the city — no warehouses of unsold stock.

## The story to tell
Clothing for the second shift. Not a side hustle costume, but the uniform of people who make things at night, show up early, and build something their own way.

## Brand values
1. **Independent** — small label, self-directed, anti-fast-fashion.
2. **Inclusive** — unisex fits, made for every body.
3. **Sustainable** — made to order, less waste, no dead stock.

## Visual mood
- Night, flash photography, film grain.
- Urban texture: concrete, subway tile, neon, bodega aisles, rooftops.
- Minimal retouching. Real skin, real bodies, real city.
- Confident but not aggressive. Streetwear, not sportswear.

## References
Stüssy, Pleasures, Only NY, Saturdays NYC, Aimé Leon Dore (early), Kith.

## Models
- 2–3 models, diverse in body type and skin tone.
- Unisex styling: denim, workwear, vintage jackets, simple sneakers.
- Hair/makeup minimal and natural.
- Capture each model's height and usual size for product-page fit notes.

## Locations
1. **Subway platform or car** — fluorescent light, tiles, motion blur of trains.
2. **Bodega exterior or aisle** — neon, product shelves, city signage.
3. **Rooftop at blue hour** — skyline, open sky, wind.
4. **Chinatown at night** — red and green neon, restaurant steam, crowd energy.
5. **LES street / fire escape** — brick, graffiti, gritty texture.
6. **Studio flat lay station** — for clean product shots and detail macros.

## Shot list

### Campaign heroes
- 1 homepage hero: full-body model in a flagship hoodie, NYC night background.
- 1 Men category hero: model in a tee, urban setting.
- 1 Women category hero: model in a tee or hoodie, confident stance.
- 1 Unisex category hero: group shot or single model in a core piece.
- 1 Accessories category hero: detail shot of hat/bag/stickers styled with a fit.
- 1 Lookbook hero: editorial group shot or candid moment.

### Product shots
For every active product:
- 1 model shot (front, full garment visible).
- 1 flat lay or ghost-mannequin front shot.
- 1 detail shot (print texture, label, fabric).

If a product has back print, add a back shot.

### About page
- 1 founder/studio shot (can be a placeholder until real founder photo).
- 1 process shot: printing press, packaging, or studio shelf.

## Wardrobe styling notes
- Layer AHA pieces over simple basics: white/gray tees, vintage denim, work pants.
- Avoid competing logos.
- Show hoodies zipped and unzipped, tees tucked and untucked.
- Accessories: simple chains, watches, beanies, tote bags.

## Technical notes
- Shoot at high resolution (min 3000px on long edge).
- Capture both horizontal and vertical frames for web flexibility.
- Keep backgrounds slightly soft so product/print remains the focus.
- Deliver RAW + color-graded selects.

## Web deliverables
- Web-optimized JPG/AVIF at 2000px and 1200px widths.
- Clear file naming: `campaign-hero-home.jpg`, `product-classic-hoodie-black-model.jpg`, etc.
- Model size info in a shared sheet for product-page copy.
